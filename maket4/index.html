“Exotic” Leather

2024-12-12

When one thinks of “exotic” leather, their first thought may be of expensive designer bags made from strange materials or advanced techniques. A wide range of animal hides fit under the category of “exotic,” including crocodile, alligator, ostrich, snake, eels, and even stingrays. The common factor here is that these hides are not easy to get ahold of and are not as common as typical animal hides, making them incredibly expensive.

Buffalo (Bison) Leather

2024-12-12

Bison leather, or buffalo as it is sometimes called, is another popular type of leather with a long history that stretches back to Native American traditions. Like cow leather, buffalo leather is produced by farm-raised animals. It is a secondary item, while buffalo meat is the primary item. Buffalo leather tends to have a higher tensile strength than cow leather and often has a grainier texture. Cow Leather

2024-12-12

Cowhide leather is the most obvious animal hide because it is so common and abundant. Valued for its sturdiness and reliability, cowhide is best known for producing everyday products like bags, belts, and heavy-duty boots. With such an abundance of cowhide leather, it is a good place to start as you learn through trial and error.

Tools

An essential toolkit should include a leather hole punch, scissors, a rotary cutter, and a stitching awl. A gluing leather adhesive is also a must-have.

Materials

Start with a scrap leather piece to practice without wasting precious material. You could experiment with cow leather or sheep leather as these are beginner-friendly options.

Techniques

Fundamental skills include stitching, edge finishing, and learning how to create patterns. You could also try your hand at wheel throwing if you are feeling ambitious!
